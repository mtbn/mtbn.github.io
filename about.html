A bit about me

I'm in my final year of a BASc in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Toronto, with a minor in Mechatronics, graduating May 2026. My academic work spans thermodynamics, dynamics, controls, and manufacturing, but I've always learned best by building real things and testing them against reality.

I gravitate toward problems that sit at the edges of disciplines, where the mechanical, electrical, and software concerns all intersect and you can't solve one without understanding the others.

Outside of engineering, I have a soft spot for classical literature, particularly Homer, Sophocles, and Euripides. There's something in how those texts think about character and consequence that I find surprisingly applicable to how I approach technical decisions.
